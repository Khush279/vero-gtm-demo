# Master Services Agreement

**Vero Health Inc.** ("Vero") and **[Customer Legal Name]** ("Customer")

Effective Date: [YYYY-MM-DD]

This Master Services Agreement (the "Agreement") sets the terms under which Vero provides its clinical AI scribe service to Customer. Order Forms executed under this Agreement reference these terms.

## 1. Services

Vero provides a software service that captures clinical encounter audio at the point of care, generates a structured draft note, and returns the note to the clinician for review and export to the Customer's EMR (the "Service"). The Service is a documentation aid for licensed clinicians. The clinician remains the author of record for every note. The Service is not a medical device and does not produce diagnostic output.

Supported specialties, seat counts, and any professional services hours are described in each Order Form.

## 2. Term and Termination

The initial term is twelve (12) months from the Effective Date. The Agreement renews for successive 12-month terms unless either party gives written notice of non-renewal at least thirty (30) days before the end of the then-current term.

Either party may terminate for material breach if the breach is not cured within thirty (30) days of written notice. Either party may terminate immediately if the other party becomes insolvent or files for bankruptcy.

On termination, Vero will, at Customer's written request, return or destroy all Customer Data within sixty (60) days, subject to the retention obligations in Section 6 and applicable law.

## 3. Fees and Payment

Fees are set out in the applicable Order Form. Fees are billed monthly in advance, due net 30 from invoice date, stated in Canadian dollars, and exclusive of applicable taxes.

Late amounts accrue interest at 1% per month or the maximum rate permitted by law, whichever is lower. Customer may dispute an invoice in good faith within thirty (30) days, and the parties will work to resolve any dispute within fifteen (15) business days.

## 4. Intellectual Property

Vero retains all right, title, and interest in and to the Service, the underlying software, models, prompts, and any improvements thereto, including improvements developed in the course of providing the Service.

Customer retains all right, title, and interest in and to its clinical data, including raw audio, transcripts, and the structured notes returned by the Service ("Customer Data"). Customer grants Vero a limited, non-exclusive licence to process Customer Data solely to provide and support the Service.

Vero will not use Customer Data to train any general-purpose foundation model. Aggregated, fully de-identified usage statistics that cannot be reverse-engineered to a clinician, patient, or encounter are not Customer Data.

## 5. Data Protection

Vero processes Personal Health Information in accordance with the Canadian Personal Information Protection and Electronic Documents Act (PIPEDA) and provincial health-information law applicable to Customer. The parties' respective data-protection responsibilities are set out in the Data Protection Impact Assessment (DPIA) and the Data Processing Agreement (DPA) attached as schedules to the applicable Order Form.

All Customer Data is hosted in Canadian regions, with primary processing in ca-central-1 (Montreal) and a warm secondary in Toronto. Personal Health Information does not leave Canada at any point in the processing pipeline.

Vero maintains an immutable, append-only audit log for every encounter and will make this log available to Customer for compliance review.

## 6. Confidentiality

Each party will protect the other's Confidential Information with at least the same degree of care it uses for its own confidential information of similar sensitivity, and in no event less than reasonable care. Confidential Information will be used only to perform obligations under this Agreement.

Confidentiality obligations survive termination for three (3) years, except that obligations relating to Personal Health Information and trade secrets continue for as long as the information remains protected under applicable law.

## 7. Indemnification

Vero will defend Customer against any third-party claim that the Service, used in accordance with this Agreement, infringes a Canadian or US patent, copyright, or trademark, and will pay any amounts finally awarded.

Customer will defend Vero against any third-party claim arising from (a) Customer Data submitted in violation of law, or (b) Customer's use of the Service outside the scope permitted by this Agreement.

Indemnification is conditional on prompt written notice, sole control of the defence, and reasonable cooperation from the indemnified party.

## 8. Limitation of Liability

Except for breaches of confidentiality, indemnification obligations, or a party's gross negligence or wilful misconduct, neither party will be liable for any indirect, incidental, special, consequential, or punitive damages.

Each party's aggregate liability under this Agreement will not exceed the fees paid or payable by Customer to Vero in the twelve (12) months preceding the event giving rise to the claim.

## 9. Governing Law

This Agreement is governed by the laws of the Province of Ontario and the federal laws of Canada applicable therein, without regard to conflict-of-laws principles. The parties submit to the exclusive jurisdiction of the courts of Toronto, Ontario for any dispute arising out of or relating to this Agreement.

## 10. General

Notices must be in writing and sent to the addresses on the Order Form. This Agreement may be amended only by a written instrument signed by both parties. If any provision is held unenforceable, the remainder remains in effect. This Agreement and the Order Forms constitute the entire agreement between the parties with respect to the subject matter.
